===============================================================================
                   MANUAL STEP 1 - ARTICLES & FORUMS (15 min)
===============================================================================

GET ARTICLES:
1. Google search: "breath control singing"
2. Open top 5-10 articles
3. Print to PDF (Ctrl+P -> Microsoft Print to PDF)
4. Save all PDFs to: Episode_01_breath/Articles/

GET REDDIT THREADS:
1. Search r/singing: "breath control"
2. Find 2-3 good threads
3. Print to PDF
4. Save to: Episode_01_breath/Forums/

EXTRACT QUOTES:
1. Upload all PDFs to Google NotebookLM or Claude
2. Ask: "Find quotes showing: generic advice, conflicting advice, incomplete 
   explanations"
3. Copy quotes to: quotes.txt

===============================================================================
                   MANUAL STEP 2 - VIDEO TRANSCRIPTS (30 min)
===============================================================================

1. Open: Clips/transcript_urls.txt
2. Pick your top 10 videos (based on titles in found_clips.txt)
3. Go to: downsub.com
4. Paste URL (one at a time)
5. Click "Download" -> Get TXT file
6. Save to: Clips/transcripts/
7. Repeat for all 10 videos

Time: ~3 min per video = 30 min total

===============================================================================
                      ORGANIZE YOUR EVIDENCE (10 min)
===============================================================================

CHOOSE YOUR TOP EVIDENCE:
- 3 YouTube comments (failed solutions, contradictions, patterns)
- 2-3 video clips (for demonstrations)
- 5-10 article quotes (generic/conflicting advice)
- 1-2 research papers (intellectual backing)

SCREENSHOT COMMENTS:
1. Open youtube_comments.txt
2. Find your chosen comments
3. Format in PowerPoint/text overlay
4. Screenshot as PNG

BOOKMARK CLIPS:
- Copy URLs of chosen videos
- Note timestamps from transcripts

===============================================================================

PATTERN EXAMPLES (5:00-7:30):
- Show video clips

STEELMAN (7:30-10:00):
- Use article quotes showing when common advice works
